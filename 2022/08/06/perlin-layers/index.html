perlin-layers
2022-08-06 #Art #Javascript

I had been using perlin noise for a number of other projects and had wondered if I could use it to make something like a topographical map. I started by taking slices of perlin noise, so for example everything between 0.1 and 0.15 is white, then 0.2 and 0.25, which gave this.

Although it is kind of neat, it doesn’t look like a topographical map, the lines are different width which is not an easy problem to solve and I didn’t bother trying to. Instead, my inspiration took me away from the idea of a topographical map and in an unknown direction, I took a single wider slice and made the slice size decrease to nothing as it got farther from the center.

I also tried doing it with distance from a ring instead.

None of these really felt right but I couldn’t tell what it needed. So I asked a friend of mine and he said it was too fuzzy, to this end I adjusted the cutoff to be non linear and sharper.

demo

click on the remake button to make another symbol and the other two to change how complicated the symbol is.

It was still a little fuzzy at times but it was reduced enough, and I thought it looked like a symbol from an alien language, which I liked, so I refined the code and made it easy to make them of any size. Here is a sheet of them.

Then I tried reversing it so it was thickest on the edge and barely held together in the center.

This was my favorite stop on this journey and I had a variant of this as my phone wallpaper for a while.
