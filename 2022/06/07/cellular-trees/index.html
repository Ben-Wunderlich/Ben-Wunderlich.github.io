cellular-trees
2022-06-07 #Art #Javascript

I was inspired by a reddit post on r/generative I can’t find anymore, it had wonderful swirling branches that created more off of it as they expanded.

To start I made it so during replication the child has direction 90 to its parent and ended once it hit any color aside from itself.

Next I modified the angle so it is TAU/6 different from its parent line.

This next one was different because I made them do tight spirals and disabled intersections so they would just keep going and spawning children infinitely, in the below image I stopped before it got too crazy.

Here the spirals are a little more relaxed, collision is very picky and children are spawned in very rapid succession.

This last one was the most difficult because I wanted a more organic cloudlike appearance similar to the inspiration, which took a lot of tweaking parameters.

demo

click a blank space to write your name, ‘stop’ to pause execution, ‘clear’ to reset canvas and ‘tighten’/‘loosen’ changes how tightly the spirals curve. The only way to start execution is by clicking on the canvas
