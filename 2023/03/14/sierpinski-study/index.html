Original

The original sierpinski triangle is a very famous fractal that can be made in a surprising variety of ways, it is a fractal because you can soon in infinitely and it never looks less detailed. My preferred way to create it is as follows

  1. at location c make a triangle around it with side length d
  2. calculate 3 new locations c1, c2, c3 by averaging c with the vertices of the triangle just created
  3. repeat this procedure at c1, c2 and c3 with side length d/3.

This produces a lot of redundant lines but I like how simple it is.

Generalizing

Another reason I like that algorithm is it makes generalizing to more than 3 sides very easy. To do that you can just make a different shape, average from those vertices and change the new side length so it fits cleanly.

First I tried doing this with a hexagon and was very disappointed.

But I had a suspicion that there was more interesting detail that was hiding in there, so instead of just drawing lines on the outside I drew each hexagon filled in but with low opacity, and I was rewarded with this.
